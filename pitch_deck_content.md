# Slide 2: The Problem

## Zimbabwe faces a diagnostic access crisis

The ability to diagnose disease early is limited by unequal access to healthcare infrastructure.

Only a small percentage of Zimbabweans have medical insurance coverage, leaving the majority dependent on public healthcare systems that frequently experience:

- laboratory equipment failures;
- shortages of reagents and supplies;
- delayed test results;
- long waiting times;
- limited specialist capacity.

For many patients, accessing diagnostic testing requires choosing between essential needs such as food, rent, transport, medication, and healthcare costs.

This creates a system where diseases are often detected late, healthcare workers must make decisions with limited information, and preventable complications become more likely.

---

# Slide 3: Why Current Solutions Are Not Enough

Traditional diagnostic pathways often depend on:

- centralised laboratories;
- expensive equipment;
- trained laboratory specialists;
- long turnaround times.

Rapid diagnostic strips provide a cheaper alternative, but manual interpretation introduces challenges:

- subjective colour comparison;
- inconsistent results between users;
- sensitivity to lighting conditions;
- difficulty scaling interpretation expertise.

A low-cost diagnostic tool is only effective if results can be interpreted reliably.

---

# Slide 4: Our Solution

# InfectoStrip

InfectoStrip is a paper-based biochemical diagnostic platform combined with smartphone-based artificial intelligence.

The system converts biological reactions into digital diagnostic support through:

1. Paper-based diagnostic strips containing biomarker detection zones.
2. Smartphone image capture through the InfectoStrip application.
3. AI-powered computer vision analysis.
4. Standardised interpretation for healthcare decision support.

---

# Slide 5: First Product — InfectoStrip UTI Plus

The first InfectoStrip product focuses on urinary tract infection screening.

The strip detects relevant urinary biomarkers through biochemical reactions that create measurable colour changes.

The AI system analyses:

- reaction zone patterns;
- colour intensity;
- spatial distribution;
- image quality factors.

The result provides healthcare workers with consistent screening support.

Future InfectoStrip products may expand to:

- InfectoStrip TB;
- InfectoStrip Febrile;
- InfectoStrip Maternal;
- InfectoStrip Sepsis.

---

# Slide 6: Why AI?

AI is not included because it is fashionable.

It solves specific problems:

## Standardised interpretation

Reduces variation caused by human colour perception, lighting, and experience.

## Computer vision analysis

Allows reliable interpretation across different devices and environments.

## Task shifting support

Enables nurses and frontline healthcare workers to access diagnostic interpretation support without requiring specialist laboratory expertise.

## Scalability

Allows multiple diagnostic strips to operate through one digital platform.
