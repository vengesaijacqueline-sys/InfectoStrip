# Responsible AI Framework

## 1. Overview

Because InfectoStrip is designed for healthcare applications, responsible artificial intelligence development is central to the platform.

The AI system is designed to support healthcare workers by improving interpretation consistency and accessibility of diagnostic information while maintaining human oversight and clinical responsibility.

InfectoStrip does not replace healthcare professionals or independently make medical decisions.

---

# 2. Human Oversight and Clinical Responsibility

InfectoStrip follows a human-in-the-loop approach.

The AI system provides:

- analysis of diagnostic strip images;
- interpretation support;
- confidence estimates.

Healthcare professionals remain responsible for:

- assessing patients;
- considering symptoms and medical history;
- interpreting results within clinical context;
- deciding on further testing, treatment, or referral.

The AI functions as a decision-support tool rather than an autonomous diagnostic system.

---

# 3. Transparency and Explainability

Healthcare AI systems must provide users with an understanding of how results are generated.

InfectoStrip will prioritise transparency through:

- clear explanation of AI limitations;
- communication of confidence levels;
- identification of uncertain results;
- documentation of model performance.

The system will avoid presenting AI outputs as absolute conclusions when uncertainty exists.

---

# 4. Bias and Fairness

AI systems can perform differently when exposed to data that does not represent the environments where they are deployed.

To reduce bias, InfectoStrip will consider:

- diverse image datasets;
- different smartphone cameras;
- varying lighting conditions;
- different operating environments;
- representation of target user populations.

Model performance will be continuously evaluated to identify and address potential disparities.

---

# 5. Data Privacy and Security

Healthcare information requires strong protection.

InfectoStrip will incorporate responsible data practices including:

- collecting only necessary information;
- secure storage of health-related data;
- controlled access permissions;
- protection against unauthorised use;
- appropriate anonymisation where possible.

The platform will be developed in alignment with relevant data protection requirements, including Zimbabwe's Data Protection Act.

---

# 6. Patient Safety

Patient safety is a core consideration throughout development.

Safety measures include:

- confidence scoring;
- identification of uncertain results;
- recommendations for repeat testing or clinical review when appropriate;
- validation before deployment.

The system will not encourage users to rely solely on AI outputs without professional judgement.

---

# 7. Responsible AI Development Lifecycle

Responsible AI will be incorporated throughout the development process:

## Development Stage

- ethical data collection;
- appropriate model design;
- documentation of limitations.

## Testing Stage

- performance evaluation;
- bias assessment;
- validation against reference methods.

## Deployment Stage

- monitoring of performance;
- user feedback collection;
- continuous improvement.

---

# 8. Commitment to Responsible Innovation

InfectoStrip aims to demonstrate that artificial intelligence can be applied responsibly to address healthcare challenges.

By combining affordable diagnostic technology with human-centred AI, the platform seeks to improve healthcare accessibility while maintaining safety, accountability, and trust.
