# InfectoStrip Clinical Validation Plan

## 1. Purpose

Clinical validation is a critical stage in transforming InfectoStrip from a promising diagnostic concept into a safe and reliable healthcare technology.

The purpose of validation is to determine whether InfectoStrip can accurately detect relevant biochemical indicators, provide reliable AI-assisted interpretation, and function effectively within real healthcare workflows.

InfectoStrip will not be deployed for patient diagnosis before completing appropriate laboratory validation, clinical evaluation, and regulatory approval processes.

---

# 2. Validation Objectives

The clinical validation process will evaluate:

- accuracy of the paper-based diagnostic strip;
- reliability of AI image interpretation;
- usability by healthcare workers;
- integration into primary healthcare workflows;
- limitations and areas requiring improvement.

---

# 3. Phase 1: Laboratory Validation

## Objective

To evaluate the technical performance of the InfectoStrip diagnostic component under controlled conditions.

## Assessment Areas

The diagnostic strip will be evaluated for:

### Analytical Performance

Including:

- biomarker detection capability;
- consistency of biochemical reactions;
- reproducibility between tests;
- stability under different storage conditions.

### Environmental Conditions

Testing will consider conditions relevant to deployment environments, including:

- temperature variation;
- humidity;
- lighting differences during image capture.

The purpose is to ensure that the strip performs reliably outside controlled laboratory environments.

---

# 4. Phase 2: AI Model Validation

## Objective

To evaluate whether the artificial intelligence system can accurately interpret diagnostic strip images.

The AI model will be assessed using labelled datasets containing:

- diagnostic strip images;
- verified reference results;
- different image conditions.

---

## Performance Metrics

Evaluation will include:

### Accuracy

The percentage of correctly interpreted results.

### Sensitivity

The ability of the system to correctly identify positive screening results.

### Specificity

The ability of the system to correctly identify negative screening results.

### Confidence Calibration

Whether confidence scores accurately reflect model certainty.

---

# 5. Phase 3: Clinical Evaluation

## Objective

To assess how InfectoStrip performs when used within real healthcare environments.

Clinical evaluation will involve collaboration with appropriate healthcare and research partners after obtaining required ethical and regulatory approvals.

---

## Evaluation Areas

### Healthcare Worker Usability

Assessment of:

- ease of use;
- training requirements;
- workflow integration;
- user confidence.

### Diagnostic Workflow

Assessment of:

- time required from sample collection to result;
- impact on healthcare decision-making;
- practicality within primary healthcare settings.

### Comparison With Existing Methods

Performance will be compared against accepted reference diagnostic approaches.

---

# 6. Human Oversight During Validation

Throughout evaluation, healthcare professionals will remain responsible for clinical interpretation and decision-making.

The AI system will function as:

- diagnostic support;
- interpretation assistance;
- workflow enhancement.

It will not replace professional judgement or independently determine treatment decisions.

---

# 7. Data Collection and Management

Validation data will be handled according to ethical and privacy requirements.

Data management considerations include:

- informed consent where applicable;
- secure storage;
- controlled access;
- removal of unnecessary personal identifiers.

---

# 8. Risk Identification During Validation

Potential risks include:

## Incorrect Interpretation

Mitigation:

- continuous model improvement;
- confidence scoring;
- human review.

## Poor Performance Under Different Conditions

Mitigation:

- diverse training data;
- environmental testing;
- repeated validation.

## User Misunderstanding

Mitigation:

- structured training;
- clear application guidance;
- defined limitations.

---

# 9. Validation Success Criteria

Progression toward deployment will require evidence that:

- the diagnostic strip performs consistently;
- AI interpretation meets acceptable performance standards;
- healthcare workers can use the system effectively;
- safety risks are appropriately managed;
- regulatory requirements can be met.

---

# 10. Long-Term Validation Vision

As additional InfectoStrip products are developed, each diagnostic application will require disease-specific validation.

The same validation framework can support future products including:

- InfectoStrip TB;
- InfectoStrip Febrile;
- InfectoStrip Maternal;
- InfectoStrip Sepsis.

This ensures that expansion of the InfectoStrip ecosystem maintains scientific reliability and patient safety.
