# Data and AI Usage Documentation

## 1. Purpose of Data Use

Artificial intelligence development requires high-quality, representative data to ensure reliable interpretation of diagnostic strip results.

For InfectoStrip, data will be used to develop, train, validate, and improve the AI interpretation system while maintaining ethical standards and protecting patient information.

---

# 2. Types of Data Required

The AI system will require several categories of data:

## Diagnostic Strip Images

Images of completed InfectoStrip tests captured under different conditions, including:

- different lighting environments;
- different smartphone cameras;
- different image angles;
- different levels of colour intensity;
- different strip reaction patterns.

These images allow the AI model to learn how biochemical reactions appear in real-world conditions.

---

## Clinical Reference Data

Diagnostic strip images will require associated reference information to determine whether AI interpretations are accurate.

This may include:

- laboratory-confirmed results;
- clinically verified outcomes;
- relevant diagnostic classifications.

Reference data enables comparison between AI predictions and established diagnostic methods.

---

## User and Environment Data

To ensure deployment readiness, testing may consider:

- healthcare worker interaction patterns;
- device performance;
- environmental factors affecting image quality;
- workflow challenges.

---

# 3. Data Collection Approach

Data collection will follow ethical and regulatory requirements.

Potential sources may include:

- laboratory-generated test results;
- controlled testing environments;
- approved clinical evaluation studies;
- healthcare partnerships.

Patient-related information will only be collected where necessary and with appropriate consent and ethical approval.

---

# 4. AI Model Development Process

The AI development workflow will include:

## Data Preparation

Collected images will be:

- reviewed for quality;
- labelled using verified reference information;
- organised into appropriate datasets.

## Model Training

The machine learning model will learn patterns associated with different biochemical reaction outcomes.

Training will focus on recognising:

- colour changes;
- reaction zone relationships;
- image patterns associated with screening outcomes.

## Model Validation

The model will be tested using data it has not previously encountered to evaluate generalisation and reliability.

---

# 5. Data Security and Privacy

Because InfectoStrip operates within healthcare, protecting information is a priority.

Data protection measures will include:

- secure storage systems;
- restricted access controls;
- anonymisation where appropriate;
- responsible data handling procedures.

The platform will be designed to comply with relevant Zimbabwean data protection requirements.

---

# 6. Data Limitations

Current limitations include:

- the need for larger and more diverse datasets;
- ongoing development of diagnostic strip formulations;
- requirement for clinical validation before deployment.

Recognising these limitations is essential to developing a safe and reliable healthcare AI system.

---

# 7. Future Data Expansion

As the InfectoStrip ecosystem expands to additional disease areas, each diagnostic application will require disease-specific datasets and validation processes.

Future datasets may support:

- InfectoStrip UTI Plus;
- InfectoStrip TB;
- InfectoStrip Febrile;
- InfectoStrip Maternal;
- InfectoStrip Sepsis.

The long-term goal is to build a responsible, scalable AI diagnostic platform capable of supporting multiple point-of-care screening applications.
