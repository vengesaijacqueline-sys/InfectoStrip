# InfectoStrip Business Model

## 1. Overview

InfectoStrip is designed as a sustainable healthcare technology platform that improves access to affordable point-of-care diagnostics while creating a pathway for long-term operation beyond initial funding and development support.

The business model combines diagnostic product sales, institutional partnerships, and digital healthcare services to ensure accessibility while maintaining financial sustainability.

---

# 2. Target Users

## Primary Users

The primary users of InfectoStrip include:

- nurses and frontline healthcare workers;
- primary healthcare clinics;
- community health programmes;
- healthcare organisations operating in underserved areas.

These users benefit from improved access to diagnostic support without relying entirely on centralised laboratory infrastructure.

---

## Beneficiaries

The main beneficiaries are:

- patients requiring timely diagnostic screening;
- rural and underserved communities;
- healthcare systems facing diagnostic capacity limitations.

---

# 3. Customers and Potential Payers

InfectoStrip has multiple potential payment pathways:

## Government Healthcare Systems

Government health programmes may adopt InfectoStrip to strengthen primary healthcare diagnostics and improve access to screening services.

## Non-Governmental Organisations

NGOs and public health organisations working in areas such as infectious disease prevention, rural healthcare, and community health may support implementation.

## Healthcare Facilities

Private clinics, hospitals, and healthcare providers may purchase InfectoStrip products to improve diagnostic capacity.

## Research and Public Health Partnerships

Research institutions and global health programmes may support pilot implementation and disease-specific expansion.

---

# 4. Revenue Model

The InfectoStrip platform may operate through a combination of:

## Diagnostic Strip Sales

Revenue generated through the production and distribution of InfectoStrip diagnostic strips.

The physical strips provide a recurring revenue model because they are consumable healthcare products.

## Institutional Licensing

Healthcare organisations may access the digital platform through licensing agreements.

## Implementation Partnerships

Partnerships with healthcare programmes may support large-scale deployment, training, and monitoring.

---

# 6. Affordability Strategy

A central objective of InfectoStrip is reducing diagnostic barriers in low-resource settings.

The platform aims to achieve affordability through:

- low-cost paper-based diagnostic technology;
- reduced dependence on expensive laboratory infrastructure;
- scalable digital interpretation;
- partnerships supporting subsidised access.

---

# 7. Sustainability Beyond Grants

While early-stage development may require innovation funding, the long-term goal is operational sustainability through healthcare partnerships and recurring revenue.

Sustainability will be supported through:

- recurring diagnostic strip sales;
- institutional partnerships;
- healthcare programme adoption;
- expansion into additional diagnostic applications.

---

# 8. Scaling Strategy

The InfectoStrip platform is designed for expansion beyond a single diagnostic product.

The first product, InfectoStrip UTI Plus, provides the foundation for future diagnostic strips targeting additional health challenges.

Potential future products include:

- InfectoStrip TB;
- InfectoStrip Febrile;
- InfectoStrip Maternal;
- InfectoStrip Sepsis.

A shared AI interpretation platform allows expansion while maintaining a consistent user workflow.

---

# 9. Market Impact

By combining affordable diagnostics with artificial intelligence, InfectoStrip aims to reduce diagnostic inequality by making reliable screening tools more accessible to communities that face barriers to traditional laboratory-based testing.

The platform supports stronger primary healthcare systems while creating a scalable health technology solution developed for African healthcare realities.
