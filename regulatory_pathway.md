# InfectoStrip Regulatory and Approval Pathway

## 1. Overview

As a healthcare diagnostic technology, InfectoStrip must undergo appropriate regulatory, ethical, and clinical evaluation processes before being used for patient care.

The objective of the regulatory pathway is to ensure that the diagnostic strip, artificial intelligence system, and overall platform meet required standards for safety, accuracy, reliability, and responsible healthcare implementation.

InfectoStrip will follow a phased development approach where validation and regulatory approval are completed before clinical deployment.

---

# 2. Current Development Stage

InfectoStrip is currently at the early-stage prototype development phase.

Current activities include:

- defining the diagnostic strip technology;
- developing the digital platform architecture;
- establishing AI model requirements;
- preparing validation strategies.

The platform is not currently intended for clinical diagnosis and will require further testing before healthcare use.

---

# 3. Regulatory Considerations in Zimbabwe

Before deployment, InfectoStrip will engage with the relevant Zimbabwean regulatory authorities responsible for medical technologies and healthcare products.

The regulatory process will involve determining:

- appropriate classification of the diagnostic strip;
- requirements for medical device approval;
- requirements for clinical evaluation;
- quality management expectations;
- post-market monitoring requirements.

Regulatory engagement will begin early in development to ensure that product design aligns with national requirements.

---

# 4. Medical Device Evaluation

The physical InfectoStrip diagnostic strip will require evaluation as a medical diagnostic product.

Assessment areas may include:

- analytical performance;
- reliability of biomarker detection;
- consistency between manufactured strips;
- storage stability;
- usability under intended conditions.

The objective is to demonstrate that the physical diagnostic component performs safely and consistently.

---

# 5. Artificial Intelligence Evaluation

The AI component requires separate evaluation to ensure responsible use within healthcare.

Assessment areas include:

- model accuracy;
- reliability across different environments;
- performance limitations;
- bias evaluation;
- transparency of outputs.

The AI system will be designed as a clinical decision-support tool rather than an autonomous diagnostic replacement.

---

# 6. Ethical Approval and Clinical Evaluation

Before testing with human participants, InfectoStrip will require appropriate ethical approval.

Clinical evaluation will assess:

- usability by healthcare workers;
- comparison with established diagnostic methods;
- performance in real healthcare environments;
- safety and workflow integration.

Clinical testing will only proceed after appropriate approvals are obtained.

---

# 7. Data Protection Compliance

Because InfectoStrip may process health-related information, data protection will be incorporated into system design.

Key considerations include:

- informed consent where applicable;
- secure handling of patient information;
- limited data collection;
- appropriate storage practices;
- controlled access.

The platform will align with Zimbabwe's data protection requirements and relevant healthcare privacy principles.

---

# 10. Commitment to Safe Innovation

InfectoStrip recognises that healthcare innovation requires more than technical feasibility.

The platform will prioritise:

- patient safety;
- scientific validation;
- regulatory compliance;
- ethical AI development;
- responsible implementation.

The goal is to create a diagnostic technology that is not only innovative, but also safe, trusted, and suitable for real healthcare environments.
