# InfectoStrip Implementation and Partnership Strategy

## 1. Overview

Successful deployment of InfectoStrip requires collaboration between technology developers, healthcare professionals, regulatory institutions, research organisations, and implementation partners.

The platform is designed to be developed and validated through partnerships that ensure scientific credibility, regulatory compliance, and practical suitability within real healthcare environments.

---

# 2. Healthcare System Partners

## Ministry of Health and Child Care

Potential collaboration areas:

- alignment with national healthcare priorities;
- guidance on implementation pathways;
- identification of suitable healthcare settings for evaluation;
- integration with public health programmes where appropriate.

The Ministry's involvement would be essential for ensuring that InfectoStrip addresses real healthcare system needs.

---

- rural clinics;

- community health programmes.

- workflow testing;

- usability evaluation;
- pilot implementation.

# 5. Regulatory Engagement Partners

Early regulatory engagement will support responsible development.

Collaboration areas include:

- understanding medical device requirements;
- preparing approval documentation;
- ensuring compliance before healthcare deployment.

Regulatory processes will be followed before any clinical use.

# 9. Partnership Philosophy

InfectoStrip is designed as a healthcare system support tool rather than a standalone technology product.

The success of the platform depends on collaboration with the people and institutions responsible for delivering healthcare.

By combining scientific expertise, healthcare experience, and responsible technology development, InfectoStrip aims to create a diagnostic solution that is practical, scalable, and appropriate for African healthcare contexts.
