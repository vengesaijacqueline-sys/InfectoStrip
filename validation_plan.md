# InfectoStrip Validation Plan

## 1. Purpose of Validation

Validation is essential to ensure that InfectoStrip provides reliable, accurate, and clinically meaningful diagnostic support before any healthcare deployment.

As a medical technology platform, InfectoStrip will undergo multiple stages of technical, analytical, and clinical validation to evaluate the performance of both the diagnostic strip and the artificial intelligence interpretation system.

---

# 2. Diagnostic Strip Validation

The physical InfectoStrip will be evaluated to determine whether the biochemical reaction zones consistently produce measurable and reliable results.

Testing will assess:

- stability of the diagnostic reagents;
- consistency of colour-changing reactions;
- reaction time;
- repeatability across multiple tests;
- performance under different environmental conditions.

The goal is to ensure that the strip produces reliable biological signals suitable for AI interpretation.

---

# 3. AI Model Validation

The AI interpretation system will be evaluated separately to determine its ability to accurately analyse diagnostic strip images.

The model will be tested using labelled datasets containing:

- diagnostic strip images;
- confirmed reference results;
- different lighting conditions;
- different smartphone camera qualities;
- variations in image positioning.

Performance evaluation will include:

- accuracy;
- sensitivity;
- specificity;
- false positive rates;
- false negative rates;
- confidence calibration.

---

# 4. Data Development and Testing Approach

AI model development will require representative and ethically obtained data.

The dataset development process will include:

1. Collection of diagnostic strip images.
2. Annotation of images using clinically verified results.
3. Division of data into training, validation, and testing datasets.
4. Evaluation of model performance on previously unseen images.

The dataset must represent the real-world conditions in which InfectoStrip will operate, including differences in users, devices, and environmental conditions.

---

# 5. Clinical Validation Pathway

Before deployment within healthcare settings, InfectoStrip will require appropriate regulatory and ethical approval.

The clinical validation pathway will include:

- laboratory comparison studies;
- evaluation against established diagnostic methods;
- assessment by qualified healthcare professionals;
- controlled pilot studies;
- regulatory review.

Clinical validation will determine whether InfectoStrip can provide reliable diagnostic support in real healthcare environments.

---

# 6. User Testing

User testing will evaluate how effectively healthcare workers can operate the system.

Testing will focus on:

- ease of use;
- understanding of instructions;
- image capture accuracy;
- interpretation of AI outputs;
- workflow integration.

Feedback from frontline healthcare workers will be used to improve usability and deployment readiness.

---

# 7. Limitations and Future Improvements

Current development limitations include:

- the need for larger clinical datasets;
- ongoing optimisation of the diagnostic strip;
- further AI model training;
- regulatory evaluation before healthcare use.

Future improvements will focus on expanding disease coverage, improving model performance, and adapting the platform for different healthcare environments.

---

# 8. Validation Commitment

InfectoStrip will follow a responsible development approach where accuracy, patient safety, and clinical reliability are prioritised before implementation.

The platform will not be deployed as a diagnostic replacement until appropriate validation, regulatory approval, and clinical evaluation have been completed.
