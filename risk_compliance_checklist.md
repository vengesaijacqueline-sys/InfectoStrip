# InfectoStrip Risk and Compliance Checklist

## 1. Overview

As a healthcare technology platform, InfectoStrip must prioritise patient safety, responsible artificial intelligence development, regulatory compliance, and ethical deployment.

This document identifies key risks associated with the development and implementation of InfectoStrip and outlines mitigation strategies.

---

# 2. Clinical Safety Risks

## Risk: Incorrect interpretation of diagnostic results

AI-assisted interpretation may produce incorrect results if the system encounters unfamiliar images, poor-quality samples, or unexpected testing conditions.

## Mitigation:

- validate the system against clinically verified reference data;
- include confidence scores with AI outputs;
- require healthcare worker review;
- recommend additional testing when uncertainty exists.

---

## Risk: Over-reliance on AI results

Users may incorrectly treat AI outputs as a final diagnosis.

## Mitigation:

- clearly communicate that InfectoStrip provides decision support;
- maintain healthcare worker involvement;
- provide user training on limitations.

---

# 3. Regulatory Risks

## Risk: Deployment before appropriate approval

Medical diagnostic technologies require regulatory review before clinical use.

## Mitigation:

InfectoStrip will not be deployed for patient diagnosis until:

- required regulatory approvals are obtained;
- ethical review processes are completed;
- laboratory and clinical validation are conducted.

---

# 4. Data Privacy and Security Risks

## Risk: Exposure or misuse of patient health information

Healthcare data requires strong protection due to privacy and ethical considerations.

## Mitigation:

The platform will implement:

- secure data storage;
- controlled access;
- authentication mechanisms;
- appropriate anonymisation;
- responsible data handling practices.

The system will align with relevant Zimbabwean data protection requirements.

---

# 5. AI Bias Risks

## Risk: Reduced performance across different users, devices, or environments

AI models may perform poorly if training data does not represent real-world conditions.

## Mitigation:

The development process will include:

- diverse image datasets;
- testing across different smartphone cameras;
- evaluation under different lighting conditions;
- continuous monitoring of performance.

---

# 6. Technical Risks

## Risk: Poor image quality affecting AI interpretation

Images may be affected by:

- incorrect positioning;
- lighting conditions;
- camera limitations;
- user error.

## Mitigation:

The application will include:

- image quality checks;
- user guidance;
- capture instructions;
- rejection of unsuitable images.

---

## Risk: System reliability and connectivity limitations

Healthcare environments may experience unreliable internet access.

## Mitigation:

Future versions will explore:

- offline functionality;
- local processing options;
- delayed data synchronisation.

---

# 7. Operational Risks

## Risk: Low adoption by healthcare workers

Healthcare workers may be hesitant to integrate new technology into existing workflows.

## Mitigation:

- involve frontline workers during development;
- conduct usability testing;
- provide training and support;
- design the system around existing healthcare processes.

---

# 8. Manufacturing and Supply Chain Risks

## Risk: Difficulty producing consistent diagnostic strips at scale

The quality of physical diagnostic strips must remain consistent during production.

## Mitigation:

- establish quality control procedures;
- conduct manufacturing testing;
- develop reliable supply partnerships.

---

# 9. Sustainability Risks

## Risk: Dependence on external funding

Early-stage health technologies often rely on grants during development.

## Mitigation:

Develop a sustainable model through:

- diagnostic strip sales;
- institutional partnerships;
- healthcare programme adoption;
- expansion into multiple diagnostic applications.

---

# 10. Responsible Innovation Commitment

InfectoStrip is committed to developing artificial intelligence responsibly by prioritising:

- patient safety;
- transparency;
- human oversight;
- privacy protection;
- regulatory compliance;
- continuous improvement.

The objective is not to replace healthcare professionals, but to strengthen healthcare delivery by making diagnostic support more accessible, affordable, and scalable.
