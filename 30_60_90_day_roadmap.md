# InfectoStrip 30-60-90 Day Development Roadmap

## Overview

The following roadmap outlines the immediate development priorities required to progress InfectoStrip from an early-stage concept toward a technically validated and deployment-ready healthcare technology platform.

The focus is on building the necessary scientific, technical, regulatory, and partnership foundations required for responsible implementation.

---

# First 30 Days: Technical Foundation and Prototype Development

## Objectives

Establish the core technical foundation of the InfectoStrip platform.

## Key Activities

### Diagnostic Platform Development

- Refine the InfectoStrip UTI Plus concept and diagnostic workflow.
- Define the biochemical detection approach and reaction zone requirements.
- Review technical feasibility of the paper-based diagnostic platform.

### AI and Software Development

- Develop the initial mobile application prototype.
- Design the image capture workflow.
- Create the AI system architecture.
- Establish requirements for image datasets and model development.

### Documentation

Develop:

- technical documentation;
- system architecture;
- data management framework;
- responsible AI guidelines.

### Partnerships

Begin engagement with:

- healthcare professionals;
- laboratory experts;
- researchers;
- potential validation partners.

---

# Days 31-60: Prototype Testing and Validation Preparation

## Objectives

Move from concept development toward technical validation.

## Key Activities

### AI Model Development

- Begin collection and organisation of training data.
- Develop initial computer vision models.
- Test image processing capabilities.
- Evaluate performance under different image conditions.

### Diagnostic Testing Preparation

- Develop laboratory testing protocols.
- Identify validation requirements.
- Establish quality control procedures.

### User Experience Testing

Conduct early testing with intended users:

- nurses;
- healthcare workers;
- potential end users.

Evaluate:

- usability;
- workflow compatibility;
- training requirements.

### Regulatory Preparation

Begin reviewing:

- medical device requirements;
- ethical approval pathways;
- regulatory processes for healthcare deployment in Zimbabwe.

---

# Days 61-90: Pilot Preparation and Deployment Readiness

## Objectives

Prepare InfectoStrip for structured pilot evaluation.

## Key Activities

### Technical Improvement

- Improve AI model performance.
- Refine mobile application functionality.
- Integrate feedback from testing.

### Validation Preparation

Develop:

- validation study protocols;
- performance evaluation framework;
- clinical testing requirements.

### Partnership Development

Engage potential pilot partners including:

- healthcare facilities;
- public health organisations;
- research institutions.

### Deployment Planning

Develop:

- training materials;
- user support systems;
- monitoring framework;
- data security processes.

---

# Beyond 90 Days: Long-Term Development

Following successful validation and regulatory approval, InfectoStrip aims to progress toward:

- controlled pilot deployment;
- healthcare worker implementation;
- expansion of InfectoStrip UTI Plus;
- development of additional InfectoStrip products.

Future diagnostic applications may include:

- InfectoStrip TB;
- InfectoStrip Febrile;
- InfectoStrip Maternal;
- InfectoStrip Sepsis.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The long-term goal of InfectoStrip is to establish an affordable AI-enabled diagnostic ecosystem that strengthens primary healthcare systems by making reliable point-of-care screening more accessible in resource-constrained settings.
