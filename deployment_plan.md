# InfectoStrip Deployment Plan

## 1. Deployment Approach

InfectoStrip is designed for phased implementation, beginning with controlled development and validation before progressing toward healthcare deployment.

The initial focus will be on establishing technical feasibility, completing validation requirements, obtaining regulatory approvals, and conducting pilot implementation in appropriate healthcare environments.

---

# 2. Regulatory and Approval Pathway

Before clinical use, InfectoStrip will undergo the necessary regulatory and ethical review processes required for medical technologies in Zimbabwe.

This includes:

- engagement with relevant health authorities;
- regulatory assessment of the diagnostic strip;
- ethical approval for clinical evaluation;
- compliance with medical device requirements;
- approval before use in healthcare settings.

Deployment will only occur after the required safety and performance standards have been demonstrated.

---

# 3. Initial Pilot Deployment

The first pilot phase will focus on selected primary healthcare settings where diagnostic limitations create significant barriers to timely care.

Potential pilot environments include:

- primary healthcare clinics;
- rural healthcare facilities;
- community health programmes;
- healthcare facilities serving populations with limited access to laboratory services.

The pilot will evaluate:

- usability among healthcare workers;
- workflow integration;
- AI interpretation performance;
- operational challenges;
- user feedback.

---

# 4. Target Users

Primary users include:

## Healthcare Workers

- nurses;
- community health workers;
- primary healthcare providers.

These users will use InfectoStrip as a decision-support tool to assist with interpretation of point-of-care diagnostic results.

## Patients and Communities

Future expansion may enable community-based screening approaches where appropriate safeguards, training, and regulatory approvals are in place.

---

# 5. Training and Support

Successful deployment requires users to understand both the physical test procedure and the digital workflow.

Training will include:

- sample collection procedures;
- correct use of the diagnostic strip;
- smartphone image capture;
- interpretation of AI-assisted results;
- understanding system limitations.

Ongoing support will include:

- user feedback channels;
- technical troubleshooting;
- software updates;
- performance monitoring.

---

# 6. Technology Infrastructure

The InfectoStrip platform will consist of:

## Physical Component

- paper-based diagnostic strips;
- packaging and storage requirements;
- quality control processes.

## Digital Component

- mobile application;
- AI interpretation system;
- secure data management infrastructure.

The platform will be designed with low-resource environments in mind, including consideration of limited connectivity and device availability.

---

# 7. Offline and Connectivity Considerations

Many healthcare environments experience inconsistent internet access.

Future versions of InfectoStrip will explore offline-capable functionality, including:

- local image processing where possible;
- offline data storage;
- delayed synchronisation when connectivity becomes available.

This approach improves accessibility in rural and underserved areas.

---

# 8. Data Management and Security

Because InfectoStrip involves health-related information, data protection will be prioritised.

The platform will incorporate:

- secure data transmission;
- appropriate user authentication;
- controlled access to information;
- responsible data storage practices;
- compliance with relevant data protection requirements.

Only necessary information will be collected to support healthcare workflows and system improvement.

---

# 9. Monitoring and Evaluation

Deployment performance will be monitored through:

- user feedback;
- system performance metrics;
- AI accuracy monitoring;
- error reporting;
- operational data analysis.

Monitoring will allow continuous improvement of both the diagnostic platform and digital system.

---

# 10. Long-Term Scaling Strategy

Following successful validation and pilot implementation, InfectoStrip aims to expand through partnerships with:

- healthcare institutions;
- government health programmes;
- non-governmental organisations;
- research organisations.

The long-term goal is to establish a scalable diagnostic ecosystem capable of supporting multiple InfectoStrip products across different disease areas.
