# InfectoStrip User Workflow

## 1. Overview

The InfectoStrip workflow is designed around the realities of frontline healthcare delivery in resource-constrained settings.

The platform enables healthcare workers to perform rapid point-of-care screening using a paper-based diagnostic strip combined with smartphone-based artificial intelligence interpretation.

The AI system supports healthcare workers by improving interpretation consistency while maintaining human clinical judgement throughout the process.

---

# 2. Primary User: Frontline Healthcare Worker

The primary users of InfectoStrip are:

- nurses;
- primary healthcare providers;
- community health workers operating within approved healthcare programmes.

These users are selected because they are often the first point of contact for patients, particularly in areas where access to laboratory specialists and diagnostic infrastructure is limited.

---

# 3. Patient Journey

## Step 1: Patient Presentation

A patient presents to a healthcare facility with symptoms requiring diagnostic screening.

For InfectoStrip UTI Plus, this may include symptoms associated with urinary tract infection.

The healthcare worker conducts an initial patient assessment and determines whether InfectoStrip screening is appropriate.

---

## Step 2: Sample Collection

The healthcare worker collects the required patient sample according to approved testing procedures.

The sample is applied to the InfectoStrip diagnostic device.

---

## Step 3: Biochemical Reaction

The patient sample interacts with the biomarker detection zones within the strip.

These interactions generate measurable biochemical reactions represented through colour changes across specific zones.

The resulting pattern contains information that can be analysed by the AI system.

---

## Step 4: Digital Capture

The healthcare worker opens the InfectoStrip mobile application.

The application guides the user through image capture by providing instructions for:

- positioning the strip;
- ensuring sufficient lighting;
- capturing a clear image.

---

## Step 5: AI Interpretation

The captured image is analysed through the AI pipeline.

The system evaluates:

- image quality;
- colour patterns;
- reaction zone characteristics;
- relationships between detected biomarkers.

The AI generates an interpretation and confidence score.

---

## Step 6: Healthcare Decision Support

The healthcare worker receives the AI-assisted result through the application.

The result is considered alongside:

- patient symptoms;
- medical history;
- clinical examination;
- other available information.

The healthcare worker remains responsible for deciding whether the patient requires:

- treatment;
- further laboratory testing;
- referral;
- additional clinical assessment.

---

# 4. Secondary User: Healthcare Administrators and Program Managers

Healthcare organisations may use aggregated, non-identifiable information to support:

- monitoring diagnostic access;
- understanding disease trends;
- improving healthcare programmes;
- evaluating implementation performance.

Patient privacy remains a priority, and individual health information will only be accessed according to appropriate permissions and regulations.

---

# 5. Future Community-Based Workflow

In future stages, and only following appropriate validation, regulatory approval, and safety evaluation, InfectoStrip may support community-based screening models.

Potential users may include:

- trained community health workers;
- public health outreach programmes;
- community screening initiatives.

This expansion would aim to bring diagnostic support closer to communities while maintaining appropriate safeguards.

---

# 6. Offline and Low-Resource Considerations

The workflow is designed for environments where healthcare infrastructure and connectivity may be limited.

Future system development will consider:

- offline functionality;
- low-bandwidth operation;
- affordable smartphone compatibility;
- simplified user interfaces;
- minimal training requirements.

---

# 7. User Training Requirements

Successful implementation requires users to understand both the physical and digital components.

Training will cover:

## Diagnostic Procedure

- correct strip handling;
- sample application;
- timing requirements;
- interpretation limitations.

## Application Use

- image capture;
- result review;
- recognising uncertainty warnings.

## Responsible Use

- understanding that AI supports rather than replaces healthcare judgement;
- knowing when referral or additional testing is required.

# 9. Design Principle

The InfectoStrip workflow is designed around one principle:

Artificial intelligence should reduce barriers to diagnostic support, not create new barriers within healthcare delivery.

By combining affordable diagnostic technology with accessible digital tools, InfectoStrip aims to strengthen frontline healthcare systems and improve access to timely health information.
