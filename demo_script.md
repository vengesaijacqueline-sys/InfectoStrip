# InfectoStrip MVP Demo Script

## 1. Demo Objective

The purpose of this demonstration is to show the intended workflow of the InfectoStrip platform, including the interaction between the physical diagnostic strip, mobile application, artificial intelligence system, and healthcare worker decision-support process.

The current demonstration represents the MVP concept and technical workflow. InfectoStrip is still undergoing development and requires further validation before clinical deployment.

---

# 2. User Scenario

A patient presents at a primary healthcare facility with symptoms suggestive of a urinary tract infection.

A healthcare worker needs a rapid screening method that can provide additional diagnostic information without depending entirely on laboratory infrastructure.

The healthcare worker uses InfectoStrip UTI Plus to perform an initial point-of-care screening workflow.

---

# 3. Demo Flow

## Step 1: User Accesses the Application

The healthcare worker opens the InfectoStrip mobile application.

The application provides:

- test selection;
- testing instructions;
- image capture guidance.

---

## Step 2: Diagnostic Strip Preparation

The healthcare worker performs the diagnostic test using the InfectoStrip UTI Plus strip.

The patient sample interacts with the biochemical reaction zones on the strip.

These reactions create visible colour changes associated with detected biomarkers.

---

## Step 3: Image Capture

The healthcare worker captures an image of the completed strip using a smartphone camera.

The application guides the user to:

- position the strip correctly;
- capture a clear image;
- minimise image quality errors.

---

## Step 4: AI Image Analysis

The captured image is processed by the AI interpretation system.

The AI model analyses:

- colour intensity;
- reaction zone patterns;
- spatial distribution of changes;
- image quality factors.

The system generates an AI-assisted interpretation.

---

## Step 5: Result Presentation

The application displays:

- screening interpretation;
- confidence level;
- relevant guidance for healthcare review.

The result is presented as decision support rather than an independent medical diagnosis.

---

## Step 6: Healthcare Worker Decision

The healthcare worker combines:

- patient symptoms;
- clinical assessment;
- InfectoStrip results;
- professional judgement.

The healthcare worker determines the appropriate next action, including treatment, further testing, or referral.

---

# 4. Key Demo Points

The demonstration highlights:

## Accessibility

InfectoStrip reduces dependence on centralised laboratory infrastructure by enabling point-of-care screening.

## AI Application

Artificial intelligence improves consistency in interpreting biochemical colour changes.

## Human-Centred Healthcare

AI supports healthcare workers rather than replacing clinical expertise.

## Scalability

The same digital platform can support future InfectoStrip products targeting additional diseases.

---

# 5. Current Prototype Limitations

The MVP demonstration does not represent final clinical deployment.

Remaining development stages include:

- diagnostic strip optimisation;
- AI model validation;
- laboratory testing;
- regulatory approval;
- clinical evaluation.

---

# 6. Future Demonstration Goals

Future versions of the demo will include:

- functional mobile application prototype;
- validated AI interpretation model;
- physical strip testing;
- healthcare worker usability testing;
- pilot implementation evidence.
